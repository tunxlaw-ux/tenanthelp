General & Financials

What are the "extra fees" I need to pay besides the rent?

‚ñº

In Nigeria, especially in cities like Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt, you will likely encounter these additional costs:

  • Legal/Agreement Fee: Usually 10% of the rent.
  • Agency Commission: Usually 10% of the rent.
  • Caution Fee (Security Deposit): A refundable fee for potential damages, typically 10‚Äì20% of the rent.
  • Service Charge: Monthly or annual fees for security, waste management, and compound maintenance (common in gated estates).

Can a landlord demand 2 years of rent in advance?

‚ñº

It depends on the state. Under the Lagos State Tenancy Law 2011, it is technically illegal for a landlord to demand more than one year's rent from a new tenant (or more than 6 months from a sitting tenant). However, in many other states, 2-year upfront payments remain a common market practice.

Legal Rights & Documentation

Am I entitled to a written tenancy agreement?

‚ñº

Yes. You have a right to a written agreement for any tenancy exceeding six months. Never rely on "gentleman's agreements" or verbal promises. A written contract protects you from sudden evictions or arbitrary rule changes.

What must be included in my rent receipt?

‚ñº

By law, a valid receipt must include:

  • The name of the landlord and tenant.
  • The date of payment.
  • The specific amount paid.
  • The exact duration the payment covers (e.g., Jan 2026 ‚Äì Dec 2026).
  • The address of the property.
  • The landlord's (or agent's) signature.

Can my landlord enter my apartment whenever they want?

‚ñº

No. You have the Right to Quiet and Peaceable Enjoyment. While the landlord owns the property, they must give you "reasonable notice" before entering for inspections or repairs, except in emergencies (like a fire or flooding).

Repairs & Maintenance

Who is responsible for repairs: me or the landlord?

‚ñº

Generally:

  • Landlord: Responsible for structural repairs (leaking roofs, cracked walls, external plumbing, and major electrical faults).
  • Tenant: Responsible for minor, day-to-day wear and tear (broken light bulbs, interior painting, or damage caused by negligence).
Always document the state of the apartment with photos/videos before you move in to ensure your caution fee is returned fairly.

Eviction & Rent Increases

Can a landlord increase my rent at any time?

‚ñº

No. A landlord cannot increase rent during an existing tenancy period. Any increase must be "reasonable" and usually requires a formal notice period (often 6 months in Lagos) before the current lease expires.

How much notice must I receive before I am evicted?

‚ñº

Except where stated otherwise in your agreement, the standard legal notice periods are:

  • Weekly Tenant: 1 week notice.
  • Monthly Tenant: 1 month notice.
  • Quarterly/Half-yearly Tenant: 3 months notice.
  • Yearly Tenant: 6 months notice.

Is it legal for a landlord to "lock me out" or remove my roof?

‚ñº
Absolutely not. Self-help eviction (removing doors, cutting off water/light, or physical harassment) is a criminal offense in Nigeria. A landlord must obtain a court order to legally recover possession of a property.

Safety & Scams

How do I avoid "fake landlord" or agent scams?

‚ñº
  1. Verify Ownership: Ask for the landlord's title documents (C of O) or previous utility bills in their name.
  2. Physical Inspection: Never pay for a house you haven't entered and inspected personally.
  3. Traceable Payments: Avoid cash. Always pay via bank transfer to a verifiable account (preferably the landlord's account directly).
  4. The "Too Good to be True" Rule: If a luxury flat in Lekki Phase 1 is priced like a room in Ikorodu, it is likely a scam.
