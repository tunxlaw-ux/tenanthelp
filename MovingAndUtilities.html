üì¶ Expert Packing Tips

Pack like a pro and protect your belongings

üéØ Before You Start

  • Start Early: Begin packing 2-3 weeks before moving day
  • Declutter First: Donate, sell, or discard items you don't need
  • Get Supplies: Boxes, tape, bubble wrap, markers, bags
  • Create Inventory: List valuable items and take photos
  • Pack Room by Room: Don't mix items from different rooms

üìã Essential Packing Supplies

Must-Have Items:
  • Carton boxes (various sizes)
  • Packing tape (heavy duty)
  • Bubble wrap
  • Newspapers/papers
  • Permanent markers
Nice to Have:
  • Plastic wrap/cling film
  • Rubber bands
  • Scissors/box cutter
  • Labels/stickers
  • Ziploc bags

üè∑Ô∏è Labeling System

Label each box with:

  • Room name: Kitchen, Bedroom 1, Living Room
  • Contents: Brief description of items inside
  • Priority: "OPEN FIRST" for essentials
  • Handling: "FRAGILE" or "THIS SIDE UP"
  • Number: Box 1 of 10 (helps track everything)
üí° Pro Tip: Use color-coded markers - one color per room for quick identification!

üçΩÔ∏è Kitchen Items

  • Plates & Bowls: Wrap each individually in newspaper, stack vertically (not flat)
  • Glasses: Wrap in bubble wrap, stuff newspaper inside, pack upright
  • Pots & Pans: Stack with paper between, fill empty spaces
  • Knives: Wrap blades in cardboard, secure with tape
  • Small Appliances: Use original boxes if available, or wrap in bubble wrap
  • Food Items: Pack non-perishables separately, label "OPEN FIRST"

üëî Clothing & Linens

  • Hanging Clothes: Keep on hangers, use garbage bags as garment bags
  • Folded Clothes: Pack in suitcases or vacuum bags to save space
  • Shoes: Stuff with paper, pack in pairs, original boxes work best
  • Bedding: Use as padding for fragile items or pack in vacuum bags
  • Towels: Great for wrapping breakables and filling gaps
üí° Space Saver: Vacuum-seal bags can reduce volume by 75%!

üì∫ Electronics

  • Take Photos: Of cable connections before unplugging
  • Original Boxes: Use if you still have them (best protection)
  • Wrap Screens: TVs and monitors need extra padding
  • Remove Batteries: From remotes and small devices
  • Bundle Cables: Use rubber bands or zip ties, label each cable
  • Backup Data: External drive or cloud before moving computers
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Never pack electronics with liquids or food items!

ü™û Fragile Items

  • Double Box Method: Place fragile box inside larger box with padding between
  • Bubble Wrap: Use generously, especially for corners
  • Fill Empty Spaces: No items should move when box is shaken
  • Mirrors & Glass: Tape in X pattern, use cardboard corners
  • Mark Clearly: Write "FRAGILE" on all sides in large letters
  • Pack Last, Unload First: Keep fragile items accessible

üìö Books & Documents

  • Small Boxes Only: Books are heavy - use small cartons
  • Pack Flat: Lay books flat to prevent spine damage
  • Don't Overfill: Leave space for easy lifting
  • Important Documents: Keep passports, certificates with you
  • Waterproof: Use plastic bags for valuable papers

üé® Special Items

  • Artwork & Frames: Wrap in bubble wrap, use corner protectors
  • Plants: Pack in open boxes, water 2-3 days before
  • Jewelry: Keep valuable items with you, not in moving truck
  • Cleaning Supplies: Seal lids with tape, pack upright, separate box
  • Tools: Wrap sharp items, secure in toolbox or sturdy box

üéí Essentials Bag (Pack Last)

Pack a bag with items you'll need immediately:

  • Toiletries & medications
  • Change of clothes (2-3 days)
  • Phone chargers & electronics
  • Important documents
  • Snacks & water
  • Cleaning supplies for new place
  • Toilet paper & paper towels
  • Basic tools (screwdriver, hammer)
  • Bed linens for first night

‚ö° Quick Packing Hacks

  • Use suitcases for heavy items like books
  • Pack a box of cleaning supplies for both old & new place
  • Take photos of furniture assembly for easy reassembly
  • Keep hardware in labeled Ziploc bags taped to furniture
  • Use towels and linens as padding to save on bubble wrap
  • Pack plates vertically like records - they're less likely to break
  • Wrap liquids in plastic wrap before capping to prevent leaks
  • Make a "load last, unload first" box for immediate needs
